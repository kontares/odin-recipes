Description

An exotic twist on the classic Puits d'amour. Fruity mango revivified by the subtly of Espelette pepper and the freshness of coriander, finished with an enticing coconut chiboust cream and crispy sweet shortcrust pastry.

Ingredients

Sweet shortcrust pastry:

  • 160g butter
  • 200g icing sugar
  • 40g ground almonds
  • 3g fine salt
  • 1g lemon zest
  • 1/2 vanilla pod
  • 263g T55 flour
  • 52g whole eggs

Egg wash:

  • 100g egg yolks
  • 25g single cream

Mango cream:

Decorate the edge of the tart with thin slices of fresh mango, glaze lightly, and add a few flowers.
  • 225g mango purée
  • 225g reduced mango purée
  • 40g egg yolks
  • 1.3g Stab 2000 stabiliser
  • 3.3g 325NH95 Pectin
  • 50g butter

Mango mixture:

  • 10g fresh coriander
  • 250g mango cream
  • 250g finely diced mango
  • 1.5g Espelette pepper

Mango confit:

  • 140g mango purée
  • 140g fresh mango
  • 35g lemon juice
  • 15g yuzu juice
  • 55g caster sugar
  • 10g glucose powder
  • 15g iulin
  • 7.5g 325HN95 Pectin
  • 0.7g xanthan gum

Génoise

  • 225g eggs
  • 165g caster sugar
  • 10g glucose
  • 150g T55 flour
  • 40g butter
  • 60g milk
  • 100g diced ginger
  • 100g mango confit

Coconut chiboust cream:

  • 140g coconut purée
  • 60g milk
  • 0.7g lime zest
  • 10g lime juice
  • 1/2 vanilla pod
  • 5g La Perruche brown sugar
  • 65g egg yolks
  • 13g Maizena
  • 14g prepared gelatine
  • 95g egg whites
  • 75g La Perruche brown sugar
  • 30g water

Directions

Sweet shortcrust pastry & Egg wash:

  1. Using a mixer fitted with a beater attachment, soften the butter, stir in the icing sugar, ground almonds, fine salt, zest and scraped vanilla.
  2. Add the flour and rub it in with your hands to form breadcrumbs, and finally add the eggs.
  3. Chill the dough for about 12 hours.
  4. Roll out the dough to about 2.5 mm thick, line the tins and blind bake for about 15 minutes at 150 °C.
  5. Brush the pastry with a thin coat of the egg wash and bake again for about 15 minutes at 150 °C.

Mango cream:

  1. Reduce the mango purée by 40% in a dehydrator or an oven at 80 °C for 1.5 to 2 hours (1 kg of purée reduced by 40% makes 600 g).
  2. Mix the two fruit purées, then add the dry ingredients.
  3. Heat one third of the purée and the yolks to 85 °C.
  4. Add the remaining purée once the mixture is cooked. At 40 °C, add the butter and mix well. Allow to cool.

Mango mixture:

  1. Chop the fresh coriander, finely dice the mango, and mix all the ingredients.

Mango confit:

  1. Heat the dehydrated mango purée with the diced mango and the juices.
  2. At 40 °C, add the sugars mixed with the pectin and xanthan gum.
  3. Bring to the boil, mix, cool and chill.

Génoise:

  1. Put the eggs and sugars in a bain-marie and heat to 40 °C.
  2. Beat with a mixer at high speed for about 4 minutes, then at a lower speed for about 2 minutes.
  3. Add the sifted flour and mix well.
  4. Pour in the butter/milk mixture at 40 °C, then mix again.
  5. Sprinkle with diced ginger and pipe with lines of mango confit.
  6. Gently knock some air out of the batter, smooth it onto a 40 cm x 60 cm tray, and bake at 160 °C for about 10 minutes.

Coconut chiboust cream:

  1. First, infuse the vanilla and zest into the coconut purée for about 10 minutes.
  2. Then make a creme patissiere with the infused purée and milk.
  3. Add the yolks mixed with the Maizena® and La Perruche brown sugar (1).
  4. Once cooked, pour in the prepared gelatine.
  5. At the same time, prepare an Italian meringue by heating the water and La Perruche brown sugar (2) to 121 °C.
  6. Incorporate the hot meringue into the hot coconut creme patissiere.
  7. Pour into 12 cm tins (about 80 g).

Assembly & finishing:

  1. Garnish the tarts with the mango mixture, add a disc of mango/ginger génoise, then a circle of coconut chiboust cream.
  2. Cover with a thin layer of La Perruche brown sugar and caramelise with a caramelising iron or kitchen torch.
  3. Decorate the edge of the tart with thin slices of fresh mango, glaze lightly, and add a few flowers.
